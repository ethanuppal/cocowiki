Twinkle-Twinkle

"sine cosine cosine sine; cosine cosine sine sine"

Twinkle-Twinkle is Mr. Cocoros's mnemonic for the angle addition and subtraction formulas of sin and cosine. It is sung to the first two lines of the well-known nursery rhyme, "Twinkle Twinkle Little Star," hence its name. Here are the four formulas:

  • sin(a + b) = sin(a) cos(b) + cos(a) sin(b)
  • sin(a - b) = sin(a) cos(b) - cos(a) sin(b)
  • cos(a + b) = cos(a) cos(b) - sin(a) sin(b)
  • cos(a - b) = cos(a) cos(b) + sin(a) sin(b)

To remember the operator between the products, Mr. Cocoros points out that "sine follows the sign."

Story with Clio

While Mr. Cocoros was tutoring a student online, he sung the mnemonic; Clio overheard him and began to imitate. Later, she sung it in a subway car while rotating around the subway pole, much to the shock of another passenger.

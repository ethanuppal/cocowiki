The Fitted Bedsheet Problem

The fitted bedsheet problem refers to the difficulty in manipulating both sides of an expression to acheive "clean" results. It comes from the common struggle of fitting a bedsheet to a bed where fitting one corner can cause the another to pop out, leading to a continuous cycle of running back and forth to attach either corner. The following example is contrived to illustrate the problem:

a = ln(4 + 7/b)

In the above equation, the left side is nice, but the right side is a trainwreck. We can manipulate the equation to make the right side clean; however, as is evident, the left side suffers:

7/(e^a - 4) = b

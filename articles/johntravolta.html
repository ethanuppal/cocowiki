John Travolta and Village People

Mr. Cocoros uses these names to describe end behavior of functions. A John Travolta is a situation where ends tend toward positive and negative infinity, or vice versa. For example, a cubic equation will be a "John Travolta like this" or a "John Travolta like that." A Village People is a situation where the ends tend toward both negative or positive infinity, for example; the derivative of parabola. In those cases, however, he also refers to them as happy and sad. Related To Paradise and Inferno, which deals with vertical asymptotes.

/bla.'hu.di.ha/

(This may be removed soon.) I do not know how to write this phrase with English orthography, so I have transcribed it phonemically in the International Phonetic Alphabet.

/bla.'hu.di.ha/ refers to the transformation of an equation in factored form into multiple equations that can be used to solve for the original equation's roots. For example, (x-2)(x-3) = 0 can be turned into x - 2 = 0 and x - 3 = 0 via bla.'hu.di.ha/.

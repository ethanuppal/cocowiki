The Family

In constructing his problems, Mr. Cocoros makes frequent reference to his wife, Helen, and daughter, Clio. When doing so, he names his actions as honoring those individuals. The latter is especially known for the Twinkle-Twinkle story. To refer to himself, he uses the nickname "Jim."

Instead of writing out the names in full, Mr. Cocoros often substitutes them for letters: he is J, Helen is H, and Clio is C.

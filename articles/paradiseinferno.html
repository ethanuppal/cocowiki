Paradise and Inferno

Source: Wikipedia

Paradise and Inferno, named after Dante's famous epic poem, the Divine Comedy, are ways of describing the nature of vertical asymptotes on one side. A paradise is a vertical asymptote where the function goes toward positive infinity and an inferno is a vertical asymptote where the function goes toward negative infinity. One can also say "double paradise" and "double inferno." Related to John Travolta and Village People, which deals with end behavior.
